Fission

Fission occurs when a neutron slams into a larger atom, forcing it to excite and split into two smaller atoms... - Office of Nuclear Energy

Nuclear reactors

Nuclear reactors are power sources that heat water to drive turbines that generate electricity. They undergo nuclear fission: the splitting of an atom.

Water is also used as a moderator to activate the chain reaction needed to generate lots of controlled power. U-235 is hit with a neutron that is slowed down by water (hydrogen has a similar mass of a neutron), and that splits into fissile material and releases more energy.
